Overview of Human Perceptuomotor Neuroscience

  • Assignment Instructions: Students are required to select a singular empirical research paper for their semester project, avoiding review articles. A bot-mediated conversation will assist in paper selection and understanding methodologies.
  • Philosophical Approaches: The lecture discusses reductionism, which breaks down complex systems into smaller parts for study, and holism, which looks at systems as a whole to understand emergent properties.
  • Perception-Action Cycle: This cycle involves the conversion of environmental energy into neural activity and back into motor actions. It starts with sensory input, such as light entering the eye, and ends with motor output, like moving a hand to touch an object.
  • Visual Processing: Light is transduced into neural signals in the retina, processed in the visual cortex, and interpreted as objects. The brain's visual processing involves multiple stages, from detecting edges to recognizing objects.
  • Motor Cortex and Movement: The motor cortex, along with the cerebellum and spinal cord, plans and executes movements. The cerebellum, with its high neuron count, plays a crucial role in motor control and coordination.
  • Spinal Cord and Central Nervous System: The spinal cord is part of the central nervous system, handling reflexes and basic movement patterns. It works in conjunction with the brain to process sensory input and generate motor output.
  • Muscles and Movement Mechanics: Muscles act as variable stiffness springs, converting neural commands into mechanical energy to move the body. The interaction between neural activity and muscle mechanics is a key focus of neuromechanics.
  • Eye Movements and Visual Perception: Eye movements are controlled by the oculomotor system and are crucial for visual perception. Eye tracking is used to study where and how the brain seeks visual information.
  • Research Methods: Motion capture and eye tracking are primary methods used to study human movement and perception. These tools provide data on body configuration and eye movements, offering insights into perceptual-motor processes.
  • Educational Philosophy: The lecture emphasizes personalized learning and the use of AI tools to tailor education to individual interests, encouraging students to explore topics that resonate with them.
